# **The Basics of The Empirical Rule of Normal Distribution**


## 1. The Overview of The Tutorial

In statistics, the empirical rule is applied widely to determine outputs. This method can serve as a reasonable estimation of the findings of the forthcoming data to be gathered after figuring out the standard deviation and before collecting accurate data.

## 3. What is the empirical rule?

68.2% of observed data will come between +/- 1 standard deviation of the mean including all normal distributions, 95.4% would come between +/- 2 standard deviations, whereas 99.7% should lie inside +/- 3 standard deviations. The above fact, often considered the "empirical rule," is a criterion that suggests where the majority of the observations in a normal distribution could be distributed.

## 4. How do we use empirical rule?

In a normal distribution, the empirical rule is applied to determine future consequences. A researcher can sometimes utilize this to evaluate the percentages of samples that fall within every standard deviation, for example. Consider the fact that the mean is approximately 10 and the standard deviation is 3.1. The initial standard deviation throughout this instance would range between (10+3.2)= 13.2 and (10-3.2)= 6.8. The second deviation would vary between the ranges of 10 + (2 * 3.2)= 16.4 and 10 - (2 * 3.2)= 3.6, and so forth.


## 5. Example of empirical rule

Assuming that an animal of a zoo survives to the year 10, with a standard deviation equal to 1.4 years. Consider the caretaker's decision to determine the chances that an animal might survive more than 7.2 years. The overall distribution of this is like this:

µ ± σ=One standard deviation which is from 8.6 to 11.4 years

µ ± 2σ=Two standard deviations which are from 7.2 to 12.8 years

µ ± 3σ)=Three standard deviations which are from 5.8 to 14.2 years

95% of the distribution, as per the empirical rule, occurs between two standard deviations. Consequently,  5% falls outside of two standard deviations, having one-half of the population above 12.8 years and the other half below 7.2 years. As an outcome, the possibility of surviving longer than 7.2 years is:

95%+(5%/2)=97.5%


## 6. Conclusion

The empirical rule is important because that allows for data predictions. This is particularly valid for big sets of data and data points with discrete values.
