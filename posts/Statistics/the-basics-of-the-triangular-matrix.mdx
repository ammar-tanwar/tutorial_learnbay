## 3. What are triangular matrices?

A triangular matrix is a specialized version of a square matrix in the discipline of linear algebra. When all of the elements just above/below the primary diagonal becomes zero then a square matrix is classified as lower/upper triangular. Triangular matrices allow matrix problems simpler to calculate, therefore it makes them vital in number theory., it is possible to express a matrix into the product of a lower triangular matrix L and  an upper triangular matrix U by using the LU decomposition approach.

## 5. What are the types of triangular matrices?



1. **Unitriangular matrix**

An upper or lower triangular matrix is described as upper or lower unitriangular when in any of the attributes  the principal diagonalâ€™s elements are all 1.

Such matrices may also be identified by the unit upper or lower triangular or, rather less frequently, normed upper or lower triangular. A normed triangular matrix, on the other hand, is not connected to the theory of matrix standard, and each unit triangular matrix is not identical to the unit matrix.

All finite unitriangular matrices constitute unipotent.



2. **Strictly triangular matrix**

An upper or lower triangular matrix is considered to be strictly upper or lower triangular when each of the variables upon its main diagonal is also 0.

All nilpotent (square matrix) finite strictly triangular matrices coexist.



3. **Atomic triangular matrix**

In addition to the elements in a single column, each one of the off-diagonal attributes in an atomic upper or lower triangular matrix is 0. A matrix with this form is therefore referred to as a Frobenius matrix, a Gauss matrix, or a matrix performing Gauss transformation.


## 6. The algebra for triangular matrix

Many techniques establish upper triangularity:



* The upper triangular matrices are upper triangular once we add them together.
* The upper triangular is the type of the result of two upper triangular matrices.
* When there is an upper triangular matrix, it includes an upper triangular inverse.
* Upper triangular is the consequence of an upper triangular matrix and a scalar.

In particular, the lower triangular matrices simultaneously generate a Lie algebra, therefore all of the above inferences remain true even if the upper triangular is exchanged completely. Furthermore, it is occasionally valid that computations combining upper and lower triangular matrices may not produce in triangular matrices.

For instance, the multiplication of an upper and a lower triangular matrix cannot always be triangular, and neither is the summation of an upper and a lower triangular matrix.


**Note:**

A Lie group is constituted by the collection of unitriangular matrices.
