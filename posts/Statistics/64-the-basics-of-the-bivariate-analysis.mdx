## 3. Understanding Bivariate Analysis

A form of statistical analysis in which other two variables are assessed is characterized as bivariate analysis. Here we have two variables which are dependent and an independent variable. Generally, X and Y are applied to signify both variables. Accordingly, throughout this section, researchers examine the differences that took place between the two variables as well as the overall magnitude. In regards to bivariate statistical methods, there are additionally univariate for one variable and multivariate statistical analyses for multiple variables.

In statistics, we sometimes examine the available set of data and generate interpretations and estimates. A study is conducted during research through attempts to determine the reason and consequences of the selected variables.

## 5. Different Types of Bivariate Analysis

The forms of variables or attributes we'll select for assessment would define the type of bivariate analyses we could execute. A numerical, category, as well as the ordinal variable, may be utilized. Logit or probit regression is possible when the independent variable can be categorical, for example, a popular brand of pen.

We can determine a rank correlation coefficient when the independent and dependent properties were ordinal, it signifies that they have a position or ranking. An organized logit with managed probit can be used when the dependent variable is ordinal.

Similarly, we might evaluate regression when a dependent property consists of either a percentage or a frequency, such as an absolute temperature. Under context for such data, the subsequent bivariate data analysis techniques are then mentioned:



1. Numerical and Numerical – This form of bivariate data comprises both independent and dependent variables containing numeric data.
2. Categorical and Categorical – When both variables do have a categorical aspect.
3. Numerical and Categorical – When there are both numbers and even a category variable.
